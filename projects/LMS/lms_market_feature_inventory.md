# LMS Market Feature Inventory

This document captures the broader feature set commonly associated with mature LMS products, even when those features are not part of the MVP.

The point is coverage, not commitment. A feature can belong in this inventory without being part of the first release.

## Market Context

Recent "best LMS" roundups and vendor pages consistently point to a familiar set of products and capability clusters. The names that appear again and again include:

- Moodle
- Canvas

## 1. Core Learning Workflow

These are the basic building blocks most serious LMS products support in some form.

- Course catalog or learning library
- Training paths or learning programs
- Modules, lessons, sections, or units
- Quizzes, tests, or assessments
- Assignments and submissions
- Completion tracking and progress indicators
- Pass/fail logic and scoring thresholds
- Certificates or badges
- Prerequisites and sequencing
- Gradebook or results views

Observed in market leaders:

- Moodle emphasizes courses, resources, activities, and progress-oriented learning.
- Canvas supports assignments, discussions, modules, quizzes, and pages.
- Blackboard highlights course creation, content management, and assessment support.
- TalentLMS supports quizzes, blended learning, and reporting tied to learner progress.
- LearnWorlds supports learning paths, assessments, and structured programs.

## MVP Mapping

These features should exist somewhere in the contract, but not necessarily in MVP scope.

- Core workflow, paths, content, assessments, and completion: MVP
- Reporting, branding, integrations, and SSO: likely early follow-up
- SCORM, xAPI, cmi5, LTI, live sessions, AI, monetization, gamification, communities: later unless they are required for the first use case
- Mobile app, offline access, and white label: later unless the product is explicitly mobile-first or externally branded from day one

## How to Use This Inventory

- Keep the MVP contract narrow.
- Use this inventory to make sure the product plan does not forget common LMS expectations.
- If a feature shows up repeatedly in the market but is out of scope for MVP, note it explicitly as a later-phase feature instead of dropping it entirely.
